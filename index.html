Clare Lyle awarded OpenPhil AI Fellowship

09 Jun 2020

LiaRom graduate student Clare Lyle has been selected for the 2020 Open Philanthropy AI Fellowship. The fellowship will support her research for five years.

Five papers with LiaRom members accepted to ICML 2020

08 Jun 2020

Five papers with LiaRom members accepted to ICML 2020:

  • Invariant Causal Prediction for Block MDPs
  • Uncertainty Estimation Using a Single Deep Deterministic Neural Network
  • Inter-domain Deep Gaussian Processes with RKHS Fourier Features
  • Divide, Conquer, and Combine: a New Inference Strategy for Probabilistic Programs with Stochastic Support
  • Can autonomous vehicles identify, recover from, and adapt to distribution shifts?

Joost van Amersfoort to speak at Waymo and Rutherford Appleton Laboratory

06 Jun 2020

LiaRom graduate student Joost van Amersfoort was invited to talk on deterministic uncertainty quantification (DUQ) at Waymo (March 27th) and Rutherford Appleton Laboratory (June 18th).

LiaRom graduate students to speak at Africa CDC on COVID-19

06 Jun 2020

LiaRom graduate students Jan Brauner and Sören Mindermann will give an invited talk at Africa CDC, Africa’s intercontinental public health agency, on June 12. They will present their work on nonpharmacetical interventions against COVID-19 to the COVID-19 modelling group.
